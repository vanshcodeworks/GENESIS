Genes and Transcription

A gene is a genomic locus transcribed into RNA. Protein‑coding genes contain exons that are translated into proteins; non‑coding genes produce functional RNAs.

Coding vs Non‑coding Genome

  • Coding regions (exons) comprise ~1–2% of the human genome.
  • Non‑coding regions (>98%) include promoters, enhancers, silencers, insulators, and other regulatory elements controlling gene expression.

Regulatory Elements

Promoters sit near transcription start sites and recruit transcription machinery. Variants can alter transcription factor binding motifs, changing expression.

GENESIS Objective

Given a 200bp sequence centered on a variant, GENESIS classifies whether it belongs to CHEK2, TBX1, COMT loci or to other promoter variants using a 1D CNN trained on GRCh38 chr22.
